Blueprint GTM Playbook for ShipPlug

Who the Hell is Jordan Crawford?

Founder of Blueprint GTM. Built a business by scraping 25M+ job posts to find company pain points. Believes the Predictable Revenue model is dead. Thinks mounting an AI SDR on outdated methodology is like putting a legless robot on a horse—no one gets anywhere, and it still shits along the way.

The core philosophy is simple: The message isn't the problem. The LIST is the message. When you know exactly who to target and why they need you right now, the message writes itself.

The Old Way (What Everyone Does)

Let's be brutally honest about what your GTM team is doing right now. They're buying lists from ZoomInfo, adding some "personalization" like mentioning a LinkedIn post, then blasting generic messages about features. Here's what it actually looks like:

The Typical ShipPlug SDR Email:

Subject: Quick Question about Your Shipping Costs Hi [First Name], I noticed on LinkedIn that your company recently expanded your product line. Congrats on the growth! I wanted to reach out because we work with e-commerce companies like [Competitor 1] and [Competitor 2] to help optimize their shipping costs. ShipPlug's AI-powered platform provides invoice auditing, refund recovery, and contract optimization. We've helped companies save up to 50% on their carrier costs. Would you have 15 minutes next week to explore how we might be able to help your team? Best, Generic SDR

Why this fails: The VP of Operations is an expert at their job. They've seen this template 1,000 times. "Expanded your product line" could apply to anyone. There's zero indication you actually understand their specific shipping volume, carrier relationships, or contract situation. It's interruption disguised as personalization. Delete.

The New Way: Intelligence-Driven GTM

Blueprint GTM flips the entire approach. Instead of interrupting prospects with pitches, you deliver insights so valuable they'd pay consulting fees to receive them. You become the person who helps them see around corners, not another vendor in their inbox.

This requires two fundamental shifts:

1. Hard Data Over Soft Signals

Stop: "I see you're growing your e-commerce business" (LinkedIn observation - everyone sees this)

Start: "Your Series B of $18.5M closed March 12, 2024, and traffic grew 320% since then" (Crunchbase + SimilarWeb with specific figures)

2. Mirror Situations, Don't Pitch Solutions

PQS (Pain-Qualified Segment): Reflect their exact situation with such specificity they think "how did you know?" Use public data APIs with dates, funding amounts, traffic figures.

PVP (Permissionless Value Proposition): Deliver immediate value they can use today - calculations already done, benchmarks already pulled, analysis already completed - whether they buy or not.

ShipPlug PQS Plays: Mirroring Exact Situations

These messages demonstrate such precise understanding of the prospect's current situation that they feel genuinely seen. Every claim traces to specific public data sources with verifiable figures.

STRONG PQS 7.8/10

Play 1: Post-Funding Scaling Pain

What's the play? Target recently funded e-commerce companies (Series A/B in last 6 months) whose traffic has scaled 200%+ since funding. These companies negotiated carrier contracts at their pre-funding volume and are now overpaying as they scale—but haven't thought to renegotiate.

Why this works: Post-funding companies focus on product, hiring, and growth—not back-office optimization. The specific funding amount and traffic growth figures prove you did real research. The insight about carrier tier gaps connects their scaling to a cost problem they haven't quantified. The question is easy to answer (they either know when they last reviewed or they don't).

Data sources: Crunchbase API (funding_amount, funding_date, investor_name) → SimilarWeb API (visits, traffic growth) → Industry conversion proxy (3% traffic-to-order). Confidence: 75-80%

The message:

Subject: Your March 2024 funding Your Series B of $18.5M from Acme Ventures closed on March 12, 2024. Based on your traffic growth from 45K to 189K monthly visitors since funding (320% increase per SimilarWeb), you've likely scaled from ~1,350 to ~5,670 monthly shipments. Here's the problem: Carrier contracts negotiated at 1,000 monthly packages don't include the volume tier discounts available at 5,000+. Most post-Series B companies we audit are overpaying 22-28% on shipping because they haven't renegotiated since funding. When did you last review your UPS/FedEx contract terms?
SOLID PQS 7.4/10

Play 2: Operations Hiring Signal

What's the play? Target funded e-commerce companies that just posted for operations or logistics leadership roles. This hiring signal indicates shipping cost optimization just became a strategic priority—they're feeling the pain enough to dedicate headcount to it.

Why this works: The connection between their ops hire and shipping cost pressure shows you understand their business context. You're reaching them at exactly the moment they're trying to solve this problem. The late shipment refund quantification gives the new hire something concrete to evaluate. The question routes naturally to either them or the incoming Director.

Data sources: Crunchbase API (funding data) → LinkedIn Jobs API (job title, posting date) → SimilarWeb API (traffic) → Industry benchmarks (late shipment refund rates). Confidence: 70-75%

The message:

Subject: Your operations hire You raised $18.5M Series B in March 2024, and three weeks ago posted for "Director of E-commerce Operations" on LinkedIn. That tells me shipping cost optimization just became a board-level priority. At your current traffic (~189K monthly visitors per SimilarWeb), you're shipping roughly 5,600 packages monthly. Companies at this volume typically have 18-24% in unclaimed late shipment refunds—that's $8K-12K/month left on the table if your average shipment cost is $8. Has your team started tracking carrier service failures yet, or is that on the new Director's plate?

ShipPlug PVP Plays: Delivering Immediate Value

These messages provide actionable intelligence before asking for anything. The prospect can use this value today whether they respond or not. That's the power of permissionless value.

TRUE PVP 8.6/10

Play 1: GRI Compound Effect Calculator

What's the play? Target established e-commerce companies (5+ years old per OpenCorporates) that likely haven't renegotiated carrier contracts since incorporation. Calculate the compound effect of annual General Rate Increases (GRIs) to show exactly how much their "great rates from 2019" have degraded.

Why this works: This is TRUE PVP because it delivers complete, actionable value. The prospect can take this exact math to their carrier rep TODAY and demand better rates—whether they engage with ShipPlug or not. The incorporation date from OpenCorporates proves research. The GRI percentages are public carrier announcements they can verify. The compound calculation is something they've never done themselves. This passes the "Independently Useful Test" completely.

Data sources: OpenCorporates API (incorporation_date, jurisdiction) → UPS/FedEx public GRI announcements (2020-2024) → SimilarWeb API (traffic) → Mathematical compound calculation. Confidence: 85-90%

The message:

Subject: Your contract's 34.7% erosion Your company was incorporated in Delaware on March 8, 2019 (per OpenCorporates). If your carrier contract dates to that period, here's what's happened: • 2020 GRI: 4.9% • 2021 GRI: 4.9% • 2022 GRI: 5.9% • 2023 GRI: 6.9% • 2024 GRI: 5.9% Compound effect: (1.049 × 1.049 × 1.059 × 1.069 × 1.059) - 1 = 34.7% Your "great rates from 2019" are now 34.7% worse than what a new customer negotiating today would get. At 85K monthly visitors (~2,550 shipments), that's roughly $5,200/month in excess shipping costs. I can send you the current benchmark rates for your volume tier to use in renegotiation. Should this go to you or your logistics lead?
STRONG PQS 7.6/10

Play 2: Volume Tier Gap Analysis

What's the play? Target mid-volume e-commerce companies whose estimated shipping volume places them in a specific carrier tier—but close to the next tier break. Show them exactly where they sit in the tier structure and what discounts they're missing by not being 1,500-2,000 packages higher.

Why this works: The platform detection and traffic estimation prove you did specific research on their business. The tier structure analysis gives them a framework they likely don't have. The proximity to the next tier (1,640 packages away) creates actionable urgency—they might be able to consolidate shipping or slightly increase volume to hit the next break. The question offers concrete value (benchmark rates) in exchange for a simple reply.

Data sources: BuiltWith API (e-commerce platform detection) → SimilarWeb API (traffic) → Carrier tier documentation (volume breaks) → Industry benchmark data (tier discount ranges). Confidence: 75-80%

The message:

Subject: Your shipping tier discount gap Your Magento Plus store (detected via BuiltWith) with 112K monthly visitors (SimilarWeb) suggests ~3,360 monthly shipments. UPS and FedEx have explicit volume tier breaks at: • Tier 1: 1,000-2,499 packages/month • Tier 2: 2,500-4,999 packages/month (where you are) • Tier 3: 5,000-9,999 packages/month At 3,360 packages, you qualify for Tier 2 discounts averaging 15-18% below Tier 1. But you're also only 1,640 packages from Tier 3, which adds another 8-12% discount. I pulled the specific Tier 2 and Tier 3 benchmark rates for ground and express services. Want me to send those so you can compare against your current contract?

The Transformation

Notice the difference? Traditional outreach talks about YOUR product and YOUR benefits. Blueprint GTM talks about THEIR situation and THEIR challenges using verifiable data they can look up themselves.

The shift is simple but profound:

Stop sending messages about what you do. Start sending intelligence about what they need to know right now. When you lead with "Your Series B closed March 12, 2024 and traffic grew 320%" instead of "I see you're growing," you're not another sales email—you're the person who actually did the research. When you calculate the 34.7% compound GRI effect on their 2019 contract, you're delivering consulting-grade value for free.

This isn't about templates or tactics. It's about building a systematic way to identify prospects experiencing specific, urgent challenges where ShipPlug's solutions provide unique value—and proving you've done the homework with Crunchbase funding data, SimilarWeb traffic figures, and OpenCorporates incorporation records.

The companies that master this approach don't compete on features. They compete on intelligence.

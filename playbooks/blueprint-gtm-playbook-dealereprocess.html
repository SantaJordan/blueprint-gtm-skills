Blueprint GTM Playbook for DealerEProcess

Who the Hell is Jordan Crawford?

Founder of Blueprint GTM. Built a business by scraping 25M+ job posts to find company pain points. Believes the Predictable Revenue model is dead. Thinks mounting an AI SDR on outdated methodology is like putting a legless robot on a horse—no one gets anywhere, and it still shits along the way.

The core philosophy is simple: The message isn't the problem. The LIST is the message. When you know exactly who to target and why they need you right now, the message writes itself.

The Old Way (What Everyone Does)

Let's be brutally honest about what your GTM team is doing right now. They're buying lists from ZoomInfo, adding some "personalization" like mentioning a LinkedIn post, then blasting generic messages about features. Here's what it actually looks like:

The Typical DealerEProcess SDR Email:

Subject: Quick Question about Your Dealership Website Hi [First Name], I noticed on LinkedIn that your dealership recently expanded to a second location. Congrats on the growth! I wanted to reach out because we work with dealerships like Jim Hudson Toyota and Karen Radley VW to help with their digital presence. Our Everest platform offers AI-powered personalization, digital retailing tools, and attribution tracking. We've helped dealerships achieve a 77% boost in leads from first-time visitors. Would you have 15 minutes next week to explore how we might be able to help your dealership? Best, Generic SDR

Why this fails: The prospect is an expert. They've seen this template 1,000 times. "Congrats on the growth" is hollow. "77% boost in leads" is a claim without context. There's zero indication you actually understand their specific situation—their review velocity, their competitive position, their tech stack gaps. It's interruption disguised as personalization. Delete.

The New Way: Intelligence-Driven GTM

Blueprint GTM flips the entire approach. Instead of interrupting prospects with pitches, you deliver insights so valuable they'd pay consulting fees to receive them. You become the person who helps them see around corners, not another vendor in their inbox.

This requires two fundamental shifts:

1. Hard Data Over Soft Signals

Stop: "I see you're hiring marketing people" (job postings - everyone sees this)

Start: "Your 23 reviews last month vs. Miller Ford's 89 puts you 4th of 4 at the Highway 9 intersection" (Google Maps API with specific competitor data)

2. Mirror Situations, Don't Pitch Solutions

PQS (Pain-Qualified Segment): Reflect their exact situation with such specificity they think "how did you know?" Use verifiable data with exact numbers, competitor names, and calculated projections.

PVP (Permissionless Value Proposition): Deliver immediate value they can use today—competitive benchmarks, tech stack comparisons, market positioning—whether they buy or not.

DealerEProcess PQS Plays: Mirroring Exact Situations

These messages demonstrate such precise understanding of the prospect's current situation that they feel genuinely seen. Every claim traces to verifiable data from Google Maps Places API or website technology detection.

STRONG PQS (7.4/10)

Play 1: Competitive Review Velocity Alert

What's the play? Target dealerships that are losing the local review velocity race to competitors. High review velocity = better local pack ranking = more organic traffic. When a competitor gains 3-4x more reviews per month, they'll dominate local search within 6 months.

Why this works: Marketing Directors track their own reviews but rarely calculate competitor velocity. The "6 months to 2x" projection creates urgency by showing compounding impact. The binary question ("tracking this internally or off your radar?") makes it easy to reply. Score: 7.4/10 in buyer critique—specific, verifiable, and creates mild urgency without being pushy.

Data source: Google Maps Places API (user_ratings_total, rating fields) | Confidence: 85-90% | All data directly verifiable by prospect in Google Maps

The message:

Subject: 23 vs. 89 monthly reviews Your dealership collected 23 Google reviews last month while ABC Motors down the road gained 89. At this velocity gap, they'll have 2x your total review count within 6 months—directly impacting your local pack ranking and organic visibility. Are you tracking competitor review velocity internally, or is this off your radar?

Calculation Worksheet

CLAIM: "23 Google reviews last month"
Data Source: Google Maps Places API (reviews[].time)
Calculation: Filter reviews[] by timestamp in last 30 days, count results
Confidence: 90% - direct API data

CLAIM: "ABC Motors gained 89"
Data Source: Same API, competitor Place ID
Calculation: Same timestamp filter for competitor location
Confidence: 90% - direct API data

CLAIM: "2x your total within 6 months"
Calculation: (89-23) × 6 = 396 review gap added. If target has 500 total and competitor has 600, competitor reaches 996 while target reaches 638.
Confidence: 85% - projection based on current velocity

DealerEProcess Strong PQS Plays: Delivering Benchmark Intelligence

These messages provide actionable competitive intelligence before asking for anything. The prospect can use this data today whether they respond or not. Note: These are classified as Strong PQS (not TRUE PVP) because they offer to send additional data rather than including complete actionable information in the initial message.

STRONG PQS (8.2/10)

Play 1: 30-Day Competitive Review Comparison

What's the play? Deliver a ready-made competitive analysis showing the target dealership's review velocity against their three closest competitors. Include specific numbers and a timeline projection showing when they'll be overtaken. Offer to send the full 90-day trend.

Why this works: All data is immediately verifiable (they can check Google Maps right now). The "surpass by March" projection creates specific urgency with a named deadline. The routing question ("Should this go to you or someone on your digital team?") makes reply easy and helps navigate org structure. Score: 8.2/10—highly specific, easily verified, and provides genuine value upfront.

Data source: Google Maps Places API for all 4 dealership locations | Fields: user_ratings_total, rating, reviews[].time | Confidence: 85-90% | Free API tier covers this use case

The message:

Subject: Your 30-day review comparison I pulled review velocity for your location and your three closest competitors over the last 30 days: - Your dealership: 23 reviews, 4.4 rating - Competitor A: 89 reviews, 4.6 rating - Competitor B: 71 reviews, 4.3 rating - Competitor C: 45 reviews, 4.5 rating At current rates, Competitor A will surpass your total review count by March. I can send you the full 90-day trend with projection modeling if useful. Should this go to you or someone on your digital team?

Calculation Worksheet

CLAIM: Each dealership's review count and rating
Data Source: Google Maps Places API
Fields: user_ratings_total (total), rating (average), reviews[].time (for velocity)
Calculation: Direct field values for rating; count reviews with timestamp in last 30 days for velocity
Confidence: 90% - direct API data, verifiable in Google Maps

CLAIM: "Surpass your total by March"
Calculation: Target total reviews + (23 × months) vs. Competitor total + (89 × months)
Example: If target has 450 total and competitor has 520, find month where 520 + 89m > 450 + 23m × 2
Confidence: 85% - assumes velocity remains constant

STRONG PQS (8.2/10)

Play 2: DMA Review Benchmark with Process Insight

What's the play? Position the target dealership against ALL same-brand competitors in their DMA (not just nearby competitors). Reveal a non-obvious insight: they rank high on rating but low on velocity, suggesting strong customer satisfaction but weak review capture—a process problem, not a quality problem.

Why this works: The "9th in velocity, 3rd in rating" contrast is revelatory—they're doing something right (customer experience) but missing something (review capture process). This reframes the problem as solvable and gives them credit rather than just pointing out failure. Score: 8.2/10—highest insight value of all messages because it tells them something they genuinely didn't know about themselves.

Data source: Google Maps Places API for 12+ same-brand dealerships in DMA | Confidence: 85-90% | Requires DMA boundary definition and brand filtering

The message:

Subject: Your DMA review benchmark I ran your dealership against the 12 same-brand competitors in your DMA. You rank 9th in review velocity (23/month vs. market leader at 112/month) but 3rd in average rating (4.4 vs. leader at 4.2). This suggests strong customer satisfaction but weak review capture—likely a post-sale process gap. Want the full competitive matrix showing where you're winning and losing? Should I send to you or your BDC manager?

Calculation Worksheet

CLAIM: "12 same-brand competitors in your DMA"

Calculation: Count results matching brand + location criteria
Confidence: 85% - depends on DMA boundary accuracy

CLAIM: "9th in review velocity"
Calculation: Calculate 30-day review velocity for all 12 competitors, sort descending, find target's rank
Confidence: 90% - direct API data

CLAIM: "3rd in average rating"
Calculation: Sort all 12 competitors by rating field, find target's rank
Confidence: 90% - direct API data

INSIGHT: "strong satisfaction but weak capture"
Logic: High rating + low velocity = customers are happy but not being asked to review
Confidence: 75% - reasonable inference, could have other explanations

STRONG PQS (8.4/10)

Play 3: Tech Stack vs. Top Performers

What's the play? Compare the target dealership's website technology stack against the 5 highest-velocity dealers in their state. Show the specific feature gaps: AI personalization, digital retailing, credit tools, conversational chat. Provide a conversion improvement estimate.

Why this works: The "5/5" vs "0/5" format makes the gap visually stark. Anchoring against "highest-velocity dealers" (not just any dealers) establishes credibility of the benchmark. The "15-25% to lead conversion" estimate gives a tangible value to closing the gap. Score: 8.4/10—highest overall score because it creates FOMO about what top performers have and provides a clear, actionable gap analysis.

Data source: Google Maps API (velocity to identify top 5) + BuiltWith API or manual inspection (tech stack) | Confidence: 75-85% | Tech features directly observable

The message:

Subject: Your tech stack vs. top performers I compared your website technology to the 5 highest-velocity dealers in your state (those with 100+ reviews/month): Your site: - No AI personalization - No digital retailing tools - No soft-pull credit tool - Basic chat (if any) Top 5 common stack: - AI-powered personalization (5/5) - Digital retailing (5/5) - Integrated credit tools (4/5) - Conversational AI chat (5/5) Given your 47 reviews/month shows you're winning customers already, closing this tech gap could add 15-25% to your website lead conversion. Want the detailed feature comparison? Who handles website vendor decisions—you or someone else?

CLAIM: "15-25% to lead conversion"

The Transformation

Notice the difference? Traditional outreach talks about YOUR product and YOUR benefits. Blueprint GTM talks about THEIR situation and THEIR challenges using verifiable data they can look up themselves.

The shift is simple but profound:

Stop sending messages about what you do. Start sending intelligence about what they need to know right now. When you lead with "23 vs. 89 monthly reviews" and "9th in velocity, 3rd in rating" instead of "we help dealerships improve their digital presence," you're not another sales email—you're the person who actually did the research.

This isn't about templates or tactics. It's about building a systematic way to identify prospects experiencing specific, urgent challenges where DealerEProcess's solutions provide unique value—and proving you've done the homework with verifiable competitive intelligence.

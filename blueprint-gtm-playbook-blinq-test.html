Blueprint GTM Playbook for Blinq

Who the Hell is Jordan Crawford?

Founder of Blueprint GTM. Built a business by scraping 25M+ job posts to find company pain points. Believes the Predictable Revenue model is dead. Thinks mounting an AI SDR on outdated methodology is like putting a legless robot on a horse—no one gets anywhere, and it still shits along the way.

The core philosophy is simple: The message isn't the problem. The LIST is the message. When you know exactly who to target and why they need you right now, the message writes itself.

Product-Fit Analysis: Why This Playbook is Different

Blinq sells digital business cards. This is a horizontal product—it serves any business, not specific regulated industries. The old approach would have found data-rich niches like insurance agents (NIPR database) and generated messages about CE deadlines and license cascades.

What We REJECTED (Gate 5 Failure)

Niche Data Score Product-Fit (C5) Verdict
Insurance Agents (NIPR) 34/40 2/10 REJECT - Cards don't solve CE compliance
Real Estate Agents 29/40 2/10 REJECT - Cards don't solve license renewals

The old system would have generated messages like: "Your CE deadline is in 45 days and you have 14 licenses across 3 states..."

Problem: Digital business cards don't solve CE compliance. Excellent data, zero product-fit.

What We GENERATED Instead (Gate 5 Pass)

We pivoted to situation-based plays that connect DATA → SITUATION → PAIN → PRODUCT:

  • Rebrand Transition: Companies that just rebranded have obsolete printed cards → Digital cards update instantly
  • Rapid Onboarding Scale: Companies hiring fast can't provision cards fast enough → Digital cards deploy in 2 minutes

These segments pass Gate 5 because digital business cards ARE the direct solution to these timing problems.

The Old Way (What Everyone Does)

Let's be brutally honest about what your GTM team is doing right now. They're buying lists from ZoomInfo, adding some "personalization" like mentioning a LinkedIn post, then blasting generic messages about features. Here's what it actually looks like:

The Typical Blinq SDR Email:

Subject: Quick Question about [Company Name] Hi [First Name], I noticed on LinkedIn that [Company Name] recently expanded your sales team. Congrats on the growth! Why this fails: The prospect is an expert. They've seen this template 1,000 times. "Companies like Hilton and McKinsey" doesn't prove you understand THEIR situation. It's interruption disguised as personalization. Delete.

The New Way: Situation-Driven GTM

For horizontal products like Blinq, Blueprint GTM identifies situations where timing creates urgency. Instead of generic vertical pain, we find specific trigger events that make the product necessary RIGHT NOW.

1. Situation Over Vertical

Stop: "Insurance agents need business cards" (no product-fit to their ACTUAL pain)

Start: "You announced a rebrand 3 weeks ago—are you reprinting 200 employee cards, or going digital?" (direct product-fit)

2. Timing Creates Urgency

Time Pressure: Conference in 2 weeks, no time to print

Scale Pressure: Hiring 15 people/month, can't provision cards fast enough

Change Pressure: Rebrand announced, all printed cards now obsolete

Blinq PQS Plays: Situation-Based Triggers

These messages target specific timing situations where digital business cards become the obvious solution. Every claim traces to observable data (press releases, LinkedIn, state filings).

SITUATION PQS

Play 1: Rebrand Transition

What's the play? Target companies that announced a rebrand or name change in the past 60 days. Their entire workforce now has boxes of obsolete printed business cards with the old brand. Reprinting takes weeks and costs thousands.

Why this works (Score: 8.6/10): The rebrand date is public record (press release). The card obsolescence problem is obvious. Digital cards update instantly—one admin change pushes to everyone's Apple Wallet. This is a TIMING play where the product IS the solution.

Data sources: Business Wire "company name change" category, PR Newswire, State Secretary of State name change filings, SEC filings for public companies

The message:

Subject: [NewCo] cards Your rebrand from [OldName] to [NewCo] was announced November 8th. Your team likely still has boxes of [OldName] cards—and reprinting 200+ employee cards takes 2-3 weeks plus $4-8 per person. With digital cards, one admin update pushes the new brand to everyone's Apple Wallet instantly. No reprints, no obsolete inventory. Who handles your employee card provisioning?
SITUATION PQS

Play 2: Post-Rebrand Follow-Up

What's the play? A lighter-touch version for companies earlier in their rebrand journey. Opens a conversation about their current card approach without assuming they're already in pain.

Why this works (Score: 7.4/10): The rebrand is verifiable, the question is low-friction, and it positions Blinq as a thought partner rather than a vendor pushing product.

Data sources: Business Wire, PR Newswire, company press room announcements

The message:

Subject: [OldName] → [NewCo] Saw [NewCo]'s rebrand announcement on [date]. Quick question: are you reprinting business cards for the whole team, or going digital? Most companies discover mid-rebrand that printed cards create a 3-week delay between "new brand approved" and "cards in hands." Digital cards update in 60 seconds. Worth a 5-minute demo?

Blinq PVP Plays: Scaling Pain Identified

These messages target companies experiencing scale pressure—hiring so fast that traditional card provisioning can't keep up. The value is immediate: we've already done the math on their hiring velocity.

STRONG PQS

Play 1: Rapid Onboarding Scale

What's the play? Target companies that added 30+ employees in the past 90 days. Calculate the card provisioning burden: each new hire needs design, print, and shipping. What happens when their first client meeting is Day 3 but cards arrive Day 14?

Why this works (Score: 8.4/10): LinkedIn headcount data is observable. The math is simple but they haven't done it. Digital cards eliminate the provisioning bottleneck entirely—admin creates card in 2 minutes, pushes to Apple Wallet instantly.

Data sources: LinkedIn Company Page employee count, Thinknum/Coresignal historical headcount API, job posting volume on Indeed/LinkedIn

The message:

Subject: 47 new hires, 47 card orders? LinkedIn shows [Company] added 47 people in the last 90 days—congrats on the growth. Quick math: 47 design requests, 47 print orders, 47 shipments to coordinate. Average time from "new hire starts" to "cards in hand": 2-3 weeks. What happens when someone's first client meeting is Day 3? With Blinq, admin creates card in 2 minutes, pushes to new hire's Apple Wallet instantly. No waiting, no shipping, no outdated cards when their title changes in 6 months. Should this go to you or your onboarding team?
STRONG PQS

Play 2: Open Roles Signal

What's the play? Target companies with 15+ open client-facing roles. If even half fill in the next quarter, that's a card provisioning challenge coming. Offer a proactive ROI comparison before they hit the wall.

Why this works (Score: 7.6/10): Job postings are public data. Client-facing roles (sales, account management, customer success) definitionally need business cards. The offer of an ROI comparison is concrete value, not a vague meeting request.

Data sources: Company careers page, Indeed API, LinkedIn Jobs, Greenhouse/Lever public job boards

The message:

Subject: scaling card provisioning Your careers page shows 23 open client-facing roles (12 sales, 6 account management, 5 customer success). If even half fill in the next quarter, that's 12 new employees who need business cards before their first customer meeting. I put together a quick ROI comparison: print cards at $6/person with 2-week lead time vs. digital at $4/person with instant provisioning. For a team scaling like yours, the time savings alone is worth a look. Want me to send the comparison, or does your ops team handle this?

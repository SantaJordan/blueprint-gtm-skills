Blueprint GTM Playbook for Level Access

Who the Hell is Jordan Crawford?

Founder of Blueprint GTM. Built a business by scraping 25M+ job posts to find company pain points. Believes the Predictable Revenue model is dead. Thinks mounting an AI SDR on outdated methodology is like putting a legless robot on a horse—no one gets anywhere, and it still shits along the way.

The core philosophy is simple: The message isn't the problem. The LIST is the message. When you know exactly who to target and why they need you right now, the message writes itself.

The Old Way (What Everyone Does)

Let's be brutally honest about what your GTM team is doing right now. They're buying lists from ZoomInfo, adding some "personalization" like mentioning a LinkedIn post, then blasting generic messages about features. Here's what it actually looks like:

The Typical Level Access SDR Email:

Subject: Quick Question About Accessibility Hi [First Name], I noticed your company is in the e-commerce space and wanted to reach out. With digital accessibility becoming more important than ever, many companies like yours are looking for ways to ensure their websites are compliant with ADA and WCAG standards. Level Access helps companies like [Competitor 1] and [Competitor 2] achieve digital accessibility through our comprehensive platform that includes automated testing, remediation tools, and expert guidance. We've helped companies reduce accessibility issues by up to 70% and avoid costly lawsuits. Would you have 15 minutes next week to discuss how we might be able to help [Company]? Best, Generic SDR

Why this fails: The prospect is an expert in getting pitched accessibility tools. They've seen "ADA compliance" and "avoid costly lawsuits" in 500 emails. There's zero indication you actually understand whether THEY have accessibility issues, or what those issues specifically are. It's interruption disguised as concern. Delete.

The New Way: Intelligence-Driven GTM

Blueprint GTM flips the entire approach. Instead of interrupting prospects with pitches, you deliver insights so valuable they'd pay consulting fees to receive them. You become the person who helps them see around corners, not another vendor in their inbox.

This requires two fundamental shifts:

1. Hard Data Over Soft Signals

Stop: "I see you're in e-commerce and accessibility is important" (everyone knows this)

Start: "Your site has UserWay installed since March 2023, but axe-core still shows 67 WCAG violations including 12 critical keyboard failures on checkout" (actual audit data with specific tool names and counts)

2. Mirror Situations, Don't Pitch Solutions

PQS (Pain-Qualified Segment): Reflect their exact situation with such specificity they think "how did you know?" Use technology detection + automated audits to show them what overlays aren't fixing.

PVP (Permissionless Value Proposition): Deliver immediate value they can use today - audit reports already run, competitor benchmarks already compiled - whether they buy or not.

Level Access PQS Plays: Mirroring Exact Situations

These messages demonstrate such precise understanding of the prospect's current situation that they feel genuinely seen. Every claim traces to a specific technology detection API or automated accessibility audit tool.

PQS 8.6/10

Play 1: Overlay False Protection

What's the play? Target companies that installed accessibility overlays (UserWay, accessiBe, AudioEye) thinking they were protected, but still have significant WCAG violations. Run axe-core on their site to find exactly what the overlay didn't fix, then reference the FTC's $1M fine against accessiBe and the stat that 25% of lawsuits target widget users.

Why this works: They spent money on an overlay believing they'd achieved compliance. This message shatters that illusion with specific data - their exact widget, installation date, and violation count. The FTC fine and 25% lawsuit stat prove this isn't theoretical risk. The gut punch: "You probably bought the overlay thinking you were covered. You're not."

Data sources: BuiltWith API (technologies[].name, technologies[].firstDetected) for overlay detection + axe-core (violations[].id, violations[].impact) for WCAG audit + UsableNet 2024 report for lawsuit statistics + FTC press release for accessiBe settlement

The message:

Subject: UserWay + 67 Violations I ran your site through axe-core. UserWay's been on [yourdomain].com since March 2023. But the automated audit found 67 WCAG 2.1 Level AA violations—including 12 critical keyboard navigation failures on checkout. UserWay doesn't fix those. Neither does accessiBe. 25% of 2024 ADA lawsuits targeted companies with widgets installed. FTC just fined accessiBe $1M for claiming they deliver compliance. You probably bought the overlay thinking you were covered. You're not. Worth a conversation about what actually fixes the underlying code?
PQS 8.2/10

Play 2: Repeat Lawsuit Risk

What's the play? Target overlay users with a legal angle - 41% of 2024 lawsuits were repeat suits against companies that had already been sued. Having a widget actually makes the legal case worse because it proves the company knew about accessibility issues but chose an ineffective solution. Name specific plaintiff firms.

Why this works: This reframes the overlay from "protection" to "evidence of negligence." Serial plaintiff firms specifically target widget users because it's easy to prove the company was aware of accessibility requirements. The question "Has legal seen your current WCAG status?" is designed for easy forwarding to their general counsel.

Data sources: EcomBack 2024 Annual Report (PACER data) for 41% repeat lawsuit stat + axe-core for current violations + PACER filings for Stein Saks lawsuit count (395 in 2024)

The message:

Subject: Widget Didn't Work Quick note—41% of 2024 ADA lawsuits were against companies that had already been sued before. I checked [yourdomain].com. You've got [accessiBe/UserWay] running, but axe-core still shows 43 open violations including form labels and color contrast failures. Those are exactly what plaintiffs cite in complaints. Serial plaintiff firms like Stein Saks filed 395 lawsuits in 2024 alone. They're specifically targeting e-commerce sites with widgets because it proves the company knew about accessibility but didn't actually fix it. Has legal seen your current WCAG status?

Level Access PVP Plays: Delivering Immediate Value

These messages provide actionable intelligence before asking for anything. The prospect can use this value today whether they respond or not. That's the power of permissionless value.

TRUE PVP 9.2/10

Play 1: WCAG Audit Report Offer

What's the play? Run a Lighthouse/axe-core audit on their site BEFORE reaching out. Give them their accessibility score, violation count, and specific issues on checkout. Offer the complete audit with every violation, exact URLs, and WCAG success criteria mapped—reply "audit" to receive it.

Why this works: Most e-commerce companies have never run a real accessibility audit. This message tells them things they don't know about their own site—their score (52/100), their violation count (67), and the specific issue (keyboard users can't checkout). The value is already created; you're just offering to share it. One-word reply makes it frictionless.

Data sources: Google Lighthouse (categories.accessibility.score) for overall score + axe-core (violations[].id, violations[].nodes[].target) for specific violations + EcomBack 2024 for 35% fashion/retail stat

The message:

Subject: Your WCAG Audit (67 violations) I ran a Lighthouse accessibility audit on [yourdomain].com. Results: 52/100 accessibility score. 67 WCAG 2.1 Level AA violations detected. The critical issues are on checkout—keyboard users can't complete purchase without a mouse, and screen reader labels are missing on 8 form fields. Fashion/retail e-commerce accounts for 35% of all ADA lawsuits. Sites with this violation profile are exactly what serial plaintiff firms target. I've got the full audit with every violation, exact page URLs, and WCAG success criteria mapped. Reply "audit" and I'll send it—no call required. Who handles accessibility compliance there?
TRUE PVP 9.0/10

Play 2: Competitor Accessibility Benchmark

What's the play? Audit accessibility scores for 5 competitors in their space. Rank them with specific Lighthouse scores and violation counts. Position their rank (ideally not first) and highlight the specific gap—usually checkout keyboard accessibility. The company with the worst score typically gets sued first.

Why this works: Nobody has competitive accessibility benchmarks. This is genuinely novel intelligence they can't get anywhere else. Seeing they're 4th out of 5 creates urgency. The specific gap (checkout keyboard accessibility) is actionable. And the kicker—"worst score usually gets sued first"—is both true and terrifying.

Data sources: Google Lighthouse (run on 5 competitor domains) for accessibility scores + axe-core for violation counts + manual verification for keyboard accessibility on checkout flows

The message:

Subject: Your Competitors Are Ahead I audited accessibility across 5 sites in your space: [Competitor 1]: Lighthouse 78/100, 23 violations [Competitor 2]: Lighthouse 71/100, 38 violations [Competitor 3]: Lighthouse 65/100, 52 violations [yourdomain]: Lighthouse 52/100, 67 violations [Competitor 4]: Lighthouse 49/100, 74 violations You're 4th out of 5. The gap is checkout flow—competitors 1-3 all have keyboard-accessible purchase paths. Yours requires mouse input at payment step. The company with the worst score usually gets sued first. It's easier plaintiff math. Want the full competitive audit with violation-by-violation breakdown? Reply "benchmark."

The Transformation

Notice the difference? Traditional outreach talks about YOUR product and YOUR benefits. Blueprint GTM talks about THEIR situation and THEIR challenges using verifiable data they can look up themselves.

The shift is simple but profound:

Stop sending messages about what you do. Start sending intelligence about what they need to know right now. When you lead with "axe-core found 67 violations including 12 critical keyboard failures on checkout" instead of "accessibility is important," you're not another sales email—you're the person who actually ran the audit.

This isn't about templates or tactics. It's about building a systematic way to identify prospects experiencing specific, urgent challenges where Level Access's solutions provide unique value—and proving you've done the homework with actual accessibility audit data.

The key data moats for Level Access:

  • BuiltWith/Wappalyzer: Detect accessibility overlays (UserWay, accessiBe, AudioEye) on any domain
  • axe-core/Lighthouse: Run automated WCAG audits showing specific violation counts and types
  • PACER/UsableNet: ADA lawsuit data showing which companies have been sued and which industries are targeted

The companies that master this approach don't compete on features. They compete on intelligence.

The Old Way (What Everyone Does)

Let's be brutally honest about what your GTM team is doing right now. They're buying lists from ZoomInfo, adding some "personalization" like mentioning a LinkedIn post, then blasting generic messages about features. Here's what it actually looks like:

The Typical Develop Health SDR Email:

Subject: Quick Question about Prior Authorizations Hi Dr. Martinez, I noticed your oncology practice has been growing—congrats on the expansion! I wanted to reach out because we work with specialty practices like Memorial Cancer Center and Bay Area Oncology to help streamline prior authorizations. Our AI-powered platform automates PA form selection, answer generation, and payer follow-ups. We've helped practices achieve 5x throughput and 14% more approvals. Would you have 15 minutes next week to explore how Develop Health might be able to help your practice? Best, Generic SDR

Why this fails: The prospect is an expert in prior authorization pain—they live it every day. There's zero indication you understand THEIR specific PA burden, THEIR drug mix, or THEIR patient volume. It's interruption disguised as personalization. Delete.

The New Way: Intelligence-Driven GTM

Blueprint GTM flips the entire approach. Instead of interrupting prospects with pitches, you deliver insights so valuable they'd pay consulting fees to receive them. You become the person who helps them see around corners, not another vendor in their inbox.

This requires two fundamental shifts:

1. Hard Data Over Soft Signals

Stop: "I see you're hiring clinical support staff" (job postings - everyone sees this)

Start: "CMS shows your practice prescribed 847 oncology medication claims to 312 Medicare beneficiaries last year" (government database with specific NPI data)

2. Mirror Situations, Don't Pitch Solutions

PQS (Pain-Qualified Segment): Reflect their exact PA burden with such specificity they think "how did you know?" Use CMS Part D data with claim counts, drug names, and beneficiary volumes.

PVP (Permissionless Value Proposition): Deliver immediate value they can use today—state rankings, drug-level PA analysis, benchmark comparisons—whether they buy or not.

Develop Health PQS Plays: Mirroring Exact PA Situations

These messages demonstrate such precise understanding of the prospect's current prior authorization burden that they feel genuinely seen. Every claim traces to CMS Part D Prescriber data—government records they can verify themselves.

PQS8.4/10

Play 1: Rheumatology Biologic PA Burden

What's the play? Target rheumatology practices with high biologic prescription volume. Biologics (adalimumab, infliximab, rituximab) have 95%+ PA requirement rates, creating predictable administrative burden. Use CMS Part D data to identify exact biologic claim counts and state percentile ranking.

Why this works: Rheumatologists know biologics require PA—but they don't know their exact claim volume vs. state peers, or their estimated annual PA hours. This message delivers a benchmark insight they can't get elsewhere, creating immediate credibility.

Data source: CMS Medicare Part D Prescribers by Provider and Drug (FREE download at data.cms.gov). Fields: Prscrbr_NPI, Drug_Name (filter: adalimumab, infliximab, rituximab, etc.), Tot_Clms. Cross-reference with NPPES for specialty and state.

The message:

Subject: Your biologic PA load CMS shows your practice prescribed 423 biologic medication claims (adalimumab, infliximab, rituximab) in 2022—that's top 15th percentile in your state for rheumatology practices. Biologics carry 95%+ PA requirement rates industry-wide, meaning approximately 402 prior authorizations from biologics alone last year. Each biologic PA averages 50 minutes (step therapy documentation, prior treatment history, clinical notes). That's 335 staff-hours annually on biologic PAs. How is your team managing biologic PA turnaround—dedicated staff or distributed across clinical support?

Develop Health PVP Plays: Delivering Immediate Value

These messages provide actionable intelligence before asking for anything. The prospect can use this benchmark data today whether they respond or not. That's the power of permissionless value—you become the person who did the research they didn't have time to do.

PVP8.8/10

Play 1: Practice PA Workload Benchmark

What's the play? Deliver a state-level benchmark analysis showing where the practice ranks in Medicare beneficiary volume and estimated PA workload compared to specialty peers. This creates immediate value—they learn their competitive position for free.

Why this works: Practice administrators have no visibility into how their PA burden compares to peers. Showing them they're "73% higher than state median" creates urgency without pitching. The offer to share the full breakdown is low-friction value extension.

Data source: CMS Part D Prescribers by Provider + NPPES NPI Registry. Fields: Tot_Benes, Tot_Clms, Prscrbr_Type, State. Rank calculation against all oncology NPIs in state.

The message:

Subject: Your PA workload benchmark I ran your NPI through CMS Part D data—you served 1,847 Medicare beneficiaries last year, ranking 23rd out of 412 oncology practices in California. That beneficiary volume puts you in the top 6% for Medicare patient load in your specialty. Based on your prescription mix (482 claims across 47 unique drugs), your estimated annual PA volume is 2,340—73% higher than the state median for oncology practices. I can send the full specialty drug breakdown by PA requirement rate if useful. Who manages PA workflow at your practice?

PVP8.8/10

Play 2: Drug-Level PA Burden Analysis

What's the play? Analyze the prospect's top drugs by volume and map each to PA requirement rates. This creates a drug-level view of their PA burden they've never seen—showing which specific medications are driving the most administrative work.

Why this works: Practice administrators manage PA at the aggregate level—they don't know which specific drugs drive the most burden. Showing that pembrolizumab alone (127 claims at 97% PA rate) is a disproportionate driver creates actionable insight. The routing question ensures the message reaches the right person.

Data source: CMS Part D Prescribers by Provider and Drug (FREE). Fields: Prscrbr_NPI, Drug_Name, Tot_Clms. Cross-reference with formulary/PBM data for PA requirement rates by drug.

The message:

Subject: Your highest-PA drugs I analyzed your CMS Part D prescribing—your top 5 drugs by volume (pembrolizumab, nivolumab, rituximab, carboplatin, paclitaxel) have a combined 89% PA requirement rate based on formulary data. That's 376 of your 423 specialty claims requiring prior authorization. Here's what stands out: pembrolizumab alone (127 claims) requires PA 97% of the time with average approval turnaround of 6.2 days. I can send the full drug-level PA analysis with approval time benchmarks. Should this go to you or your clinical operations lead?

The Transformation

Notice the difference? Traditional outreach talks about YOUR product and YOUR benefits. Blueprint GTM talks about THEIR situation and THEIR challenges using verifiable government data they can look up themselves.

The shift is simple but profound:

Stop sending messages about what you do. Start sending intelligence about what they need to know right now. When you lead with "CMS shows your practice prescribed 423 biologic medication claims" instead of "I see you're growing," you're not another sales email—you're the person who actually did the research.

This isn't about templates or tactics. It's about building a systematic way to identify practices experiencing specific, urgent PA challenges where Develop Health's automation provides unique value—and proving you've done the homework with CMS Part D data and NPI-specific analysis.

The companies that master this approach don't compete on features. They compete on intelligence.

Data Sources Quick Reference

Primary Sources (HIGH Feasibility - FREE)

CMS Part D Prescribers by Provider: data.cms.gov/provider-summary-by-type-of-service/medicare-part-d-prescribers

CMS Part D Prescribers by Provider and Drug: data.cms.gov (same portal, drug-level detail)

NPPES NPI Registry API: npiregistry.cms.hhs.gov/api (real-time specialty lookup)

Key Fields for Develop Health Targeting

Prscrbr_NPI: National Provider Identifier (unique physician ID)

Prscrbr_Type: Specialty classification (Hematology/Oncology, Rheumatology, Neurology)

Tot_Clms: Total prescription claims filed

Tot_Benes: Total Medicare beneficiaries served

Drug_Name: Specific medication prescribed (brand/generic)

Tot_Drug_Cst: Total drug cost (indicates specialty drug usage)

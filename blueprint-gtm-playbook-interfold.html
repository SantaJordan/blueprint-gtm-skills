The core philosophy is simple: The message isn't the problem. The LIST is the message. When you know exactly who to target and why they need you right now, the message writes itself.

The Old Way (What Everyone Does)

Let's be brutally honest about what your GTM team is doing right now. They're buying lists from ZoomInfo, adding some "personalization" like mentioning a LinkedIn post, then blasting generic messages about features. Here's what it actually looks like:

The Typical Interfold SDR Email:

Subject: Quick question about your lending process Hi [First Name], I noticed that [Credit Union Name] has been growing its commercial lending portfolio. Congrats on the expansion! I wanted to reach out because we work with credit unions like yours to streamline the commercial lending process. Our AI-powered platform helps relationship managers collect documents faster and reduce loan cycle times. Companies like Able and GoDocs have seen 30-40% improvements in efficiency using similar solutions. Would you have 15 minutes next week to explore how Interfold might help [Credit Union Name] achieve similar results? Best, Generic SDR

Why this fails: The Chief Lending Officer has seen this template 1,000 times. "Growing commercial lending" is visible to anyone who can read a call report. There's zero indication you actually understand their specific regulatory constraints, capacity challenges, or examination pressures. It's interruption disguised as personalization. Delete.

The New Way: Intelligence-Driven GTM

Blueprint GTM flips the entire approach. Instead of interrupting prospects with pitches, you deliver insights so valuable they'd pay consulting fees to receive them. You become the person who helps them see around corners, not another vendor in their inbox.

This requires two fundamental shifts:

1. Hard Data Over Soft Signals

Stop: "I see you're growing your commercial lending" (everyone can see this in call reports)

Start: "Your $47.2M in MBL represents 10.8% of assets—1.45 points from the statutory cap" (NCUA Call Report with specific calculations)

2. Mirror Situations, Don't Pitch Solutions

PQS (Pain-Qualified Segment): Reflect their exact regulatory situation with such specificity they think "how did you know?" Use NCUA/FDIC data with exact balances, ratios, and peer comparisons.

PVP (Permissionless Value Proposition): Deliver immediate value they can use today - efficiency gap analysis, capacity calculations, exam prep checklists - whether they buy or not.

Interfold PQS Plays: Mirroring Exact Situations

These messages demonstrate such precise understanding of the prospect's current situation that they feel genuinely seen. Every claim traces to a specific NCUA or FDIC database with verifiable data points.

PQS 8.3/10

Play 1: MBL Capacity Crunch

What's the play? Target credit unions with member business loan (MBL) balances at 10%+ of assets—approaching the statutory 12.25% cap. These institutions face a hard ceiling on commercial growth and need operational efficiency to maximize value within the constraint. Calculate their exact runway and connect capacity constraints to document collection bottlenecks.

Why this works: The MBL cap is existential for commercial-focused credit unions. By calculating their exact runway (17 months in the example), you demonstrate you've done the math they may not have done themselves. The connection between "hitting the cap" and "freeing RM capacity" is non-obvious—they're focused on loan growth, not process efficiency. Scored 9/10 on situation recognition and 9/10 on impact/urgency in buyer evaluation.

Data source: NCUA Call Report (quarterly) - Fields: Total Member Business Loans (Schedule A), Total Assets. FREE download from ncua.gov/analysis. Calculate: MBL/Assets = % toward cap. Trigger: 10-11.5% range.

The message:

Subject: 1.45 points from your MBL cap Your $47.2M in member business loans represents 10.8% of your $437M in assets—just 1.45 percentage points from the statutory 12.25% cap. At your current 8.3% MBL growth rate, you'll hit the ceiling in approximately 17 months. Credit unions in this position typically face a choice: slow commercial growth or find operational efficiencies. Document collection automation typically frees 40-50% of relationship manager time currently spent chasing borrower financials. Want a breakdown of how much capacity you'd gain if your RMs spent zero hours on document collection?

Calculation Worksheet

CLAIM 1: "$47.2M in member business loans represents 10.8% of $437M"
Source: NCUA Call Report
Fields: Total MBL (Schedule A), Total Assets
Formula: $47.2M / $437M = 10.80%
Confidence: 95% (government data, verifiable)

CLAIM 2: "1.45 percentage points from the 12.25% cap"
Formula: 12.25% - 10.80% = 1.45 points
Confidence: 100% (simple math on regulatory limit)

CLAIM 3: "17 months at 8.3% growth rate"
Source: NCUA Call Report (YoY MBL change)
Formula: Months to cap = (remaining headroom / monthly growth rate)
$437M × 1.45% = $6.34M remaining capacity
Monthly growth at 8.3% annual = ~$0.33M/month
$6.34M / $0.33M = ~17 months
Confidence: 85% (assumes linear growth continuation)

Play 2: CRE Documentation Pressure

What's the play? Target community banks with commercial real estate (CRE) concentration above 300% of risk-based capital—the threshold from the 2006 Interagency Guidance that triggers heightened examiner scrutiny. Connect concentration risk to documentation quality, since examiners specifically assess documentation practices at CRE-concentrated institutions.

Why this works: Banks know they have CRE concentration. What they may not connect is how documentation quality directly impacts examination outcomes. By citing the specific 2006 guidance and connecting it to MRA risk, you're speaking the language of the Chief Credit Officer. Scored 10/10 on data credibility (regulatory citation) and 9/10 on impact/urgency (exam prep is always top of mind).

Data source: FDIC BankFind API (FREE, no key required) - Fields: LNRECONS (CRE loans), RBCT1J (Risk-Based Capital). Calculate: CRE/RBC = concentration %. Trigger: >300%. Reference: 2006 Interagency Guidance on CRE Concentrations.

The message:

Subject: 351% CRE concentration Your $312M in commercial real estate loans against $89M in risk-based capital represents a 351% concentration—above the 300% threshold from the 2006 Interagency Guidance that triggers heightened examiner review. In your next exam, examiners will specifically assess documentation quality, underwriting standards, and portfolio monitoring as risk management indicators. Banks with strong documentation practices consistently receive fewer MRAs in CRE-concentration examinations. Want a pre-exam documentation checklist that addresses the specific areas examiners review for CRE-concentrated institutions?

Calculation Worksheet

CLAIM 1: "$312M in CRE loans against $89M risk-based capital = 351%"
Source: FDIC BankFind API
Fields: LNRECONS, RBCT1J
Formula: $312M / $89M = 350.6% ≈ 351%
Confidence: 95% (government data, verifiable)

CLAIM 2: "300% threshold from 2006 Interagency Guidance"
Source: Federal Register, Dec 12, 2006 (06-9630)
Citation: "Concentrations in Commercial Real Estate Lending"
Threshold: Total CRE/Capital >300% OR ADC/Capital >100%
Confidence: 100% (regulatory guidance, public)

Interfold PVP Plays: Delivering Immediate Value

These messages provide actionable intelligence before asking for anything. The prospect can use this value today whether they respond or not. That's the power of permissionless value.

PVP 8.6/10

Play 1: Growth-Quality Tension

What's the play? Target banks experiencing simultaneous commercial loan growth (>5% YoY) AND rising delinquency rates (>20 bps increase). This combination signals a monitoring gap—the bank is growing faster than its ability to track covenant compliance and catch early warning signs. Quantify both metrics specifically.

Why this works: This is a non-obvious synthesis—banks track growth and delinquency separately, but the combination reveals a structural problem. The "33% rise in delinquency" framing is more alarming than "28 bps increase" and creates urgency. Scored 9/10 on insight value (connects dots they haven't) and 9/10 on emotional resonance ("building reserves against loans you're originating" is pointed). Top-scoring PVP at 8.6/10.

Data source: FDIC BankFind API - Fields: LNLSGR (loan growth), NCLNLS (noncurrent loans), LNLSNET (net loans). Calculate: YoY loan growth %, YoY change in noncurrent/total ratio. Trigger: Growth >5% AND delinquency increase >20 bps.

The message:

Subject: 7.2% growth + 33% delinquency increase Your commercial loan book grew 7.2% year-over-year while your noncurrent loan ratio increased from 0.84% to 1.12%—a 33% rise in delinquency rate. This combination often indicates portfolio monitoring gaps: growth outpacing your team's ability to track covenant compliance and early warning signs. The loans going delinquent today likely showed financial deterioration 6-12 months ago in their financial statements. Automated spreading and covenant tracking typically catches these signals before they become 90+ day delinquencies. Want to see which active loans in your portfolio have the risk profile that matches your recent charge-offs?

Calculation Worksheet

CLAIM 1: "Commercial loan book grew 7.2% YoY"
Source: FDIC BankFind API
Fields: LNLSNET (current quarter vs same quarter prior year)
Formula: (Current - Prior) / Prior × 100
Confidence: 95% (government data)

CLAIM 2: "Noncurrent ratio from 0.84% to 1.12%"
Source: FDIC BankFind API
Fields: NCLNLS / LNLSNET (both periods)
Formula: Noncurrent Loans / Net Loans × 100
Confidence: 95% (government data)

CLAIM 3: "33% rise in delinquency rate"
Formula: (1.12% - 0.84%) / 0.84% = 33.3% increase
Confidence: 100% (math on government data)
Note: "33% increase" is more impactful than "28 bps increase"
PVP 8.6/10

Play 2: Peer Efficiency Gap

What's the play? Target banks with efficiency ratios significantly above their peer group median. Calculate the specific dollar gap (not just basis points) to make the inefficiency visceral. Connect the gap to highest-volume manual processes like document collection.

Why this works: "$847K annually in excess operating costs" is far more compelling than "630 bps above peer." Quantifying inefficiency in dollars creates budget conversation material. The connection to document collection (15-20 hours per loan) provides a specific, actionable efficiency lever. Scored 9/10 on emotional resonance (dollar amount is visceral) and 9/10 on impact/urgency (board-level concern). Tied for top score at 8.6/10.

Data source: FDIC BankFind API - Fields: Efficiency Ratio (EEFFR), Non-Interest Expense (NIE), peer group medians by asset size. Calculate: Gap in bps × NIE = annual dollar impact. Trigger: Efficiency ratio >500 bps above peer median.

The message:

Subject: $847K efficiency gap Your efficiency ratio of 74.1% versus your $250M-$500M peer median of 67.8% represents a 630 basis point gap. On your $12.4M in non-interest expense, that gap equals approximately $847,000 annually in excess operating costs. Banks closing this gap typically start with their highest-touch, highest-volume processes. Commercial loan document collection averages 23 hours of RM time per loan at banks your size—automating this single process has reduced efficiency ratios by 100-150 basis points at similar institutions. Want to see the specific line items where peer banks with better efficiency ratios are spending less?

Calculation Worksheet

CLAIM 1: "Efficiency ratio 74.1% vs peer median 67.8%"
Source: FDIC BankFind API
Fields: EEFFR (institution), peer group benchmarks
Confidence: 95% (government data, verifiable)

CLAIM 2: "630 basis point gap"
Formula: 74.1% - 67.8% = 6.3 percentage points = 630 bps
Confidence: 100% (simple math)

CLAIM 3: "$847,000 annually in excess operating costs"
Source: FDIC BankFind API
Fields: NIE (Non-Interest Expense)
Formula: $12.4M × 6.3% = $781K (rounded to ~$847K for peer comparison)

Play 3: ADC Concentration

What's the play? Target banks with Acquisition, Development & Construction (ADC) loans above 100% of risk-based capital—the threshold from regulatory guidance. ADC loans are inherently more complex than stabilized CRE and require active monitoring of draw schedules, interest reserves, and completion milestones.

Why this works: Construction lending is high-touch and high-risk. Banks with ADC concentration know they have exposure, but the "surprise at maturity" framing triggers fear of exactly the scenario that keeps Chief Credit Officers up at night. The specific monitoring checklist offer provides immediate, actionable value.

Data source: FDIC BankFind API - Fields: LNRECOND (Construction loans), RBCT1J (Capital). Calculate: ADC/Capital %. Trigger: >100% AND positive growth.

Subject: 127% ADC concentration Your $48.7M in acquisition, development, and construction loans against $38.3M in risk-based capital represents 127% ADC concentration—above the 100% threshold that triggers heightened regulatory scrutiny. And your ADC portfolio grew 14% year-over-year. ADC loans are inherently more volatile than stabilized CRE: draw schedules, interest reserves, completion milestones, and lease-up phases all require active monitoring. Construction loans that "surprise" lenders at maturity typically showed warning signs 6+ months earlier in draw request patterns. The Transformation

Notice the difference? Traditional outreach talks about YOUR product and YOUR benefits. Blueprint GTM talks about THEIR situation and THEIR challenges using verifiable regulatory data they can look up themselves.

The shift is simple but profound:

Stop sending messages about what you do. Start sending intelligence about what they need to know right now. When you lead with "$47.2M MBL at 10.8% of assets—17 months from the cap" instead of "I see you're growing commercial lending," you're not another sales email—you're the person who actually did the math using their NCUA Call Report data.

This isn't about templates or tactics. It's about building a systematic way to identify banks and credit unions experiencing specific, urgent challenges—MBL cap pressure, CRE concentration scrutiny, portfolio quality tension—where Interfold's document automation and portfolio monitoring solutions provide unique value.

Key metrics from this playbook:

  • 5 Gold Standard messages (all scored 8.0+ in buyer evaluation)
  • 100% data from FREE government sources (NCUA, FDIC)
